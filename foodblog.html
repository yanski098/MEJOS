Alimango is a Filipino term for mud crab. There are various ways to prepare alimango dishes in Filipino cuisine, such as steaming, grilling, or cooking in sauces like sweet and sour or coconut milk-based sauces. Each preparation method adds its own unique flavor profile to the dish. The texture of alimango, or mud crab meat, can be described as succulent, tender, and slightly firm. When cooked proparely, the meat has a delicate yet substantial texture with a sweet flavor.
